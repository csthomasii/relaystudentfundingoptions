A comprehensive guide to reducing the cost of your certification and master’s degree program at Relay Graduate School of Education. Covers federal programs and state-specific programs across all Relay locations.

How to Use This Guide

This guide is designed specifically for students enrolled in—or considering enrollment in—teacher preparation programs at Relay Graduate School of Education, including Master of Arts in Teaching (MAT) programs, the Relay Teaching Residency, Advanced Certificate in Special Education, and Alternative Certification programs.

Programs are organized into two categories: federal programs available to Relay students nationwide, and state-specific programs for each state where Relay operates. Within each category, we distinguish between programs you can access during enrollment and those available after graduation once you are teaching.

Important: Only programs available to students at Relay Graduate School of Education are included. Programs that require enrollment at a different institution have been excluded. All programs listed are compatible with Relay’s accredited, nonprofit status as an institution of higher education.

Program availability, funding levels, and eligibility requirements change frequently. Many state programs depend on annual legislative appropriations. Federal student loan policy is in active flux—PSLF has new rules effective July 1, 2026, and IDR plans are being restructured through 2028. Always verify current status with the administering agency and Relay’s financial aid office before making financial decisions.

Federal Programs: During Enrollment

These programs can help reduce costs while you are actively enrolled at Relay.

TEACH Grant

~$1,800/yr (part-time) Grant ▼
AmountUp to $4,000 per academic year for full-time students; $8,000 aggregate maximum for graduate students. For Relay’s part-time students, the typical award is approximately $1,800 per year (~$3,600 over a two-year program) due to enrollment timing and credit load.
EligibilityEnrolled in a TEACH Grant-eligible program at a participating institution; maintain a 3.25+ cumulative GPA (undergrad GPA applies for your first semester at Relay); complete FAFSA and TEACH Grant counseling annually.
ObligationAgree to teach full-time for at least 4 years in a high-need field at a low-income school within 8 years of program completion. If the obligation is not met, the grant converts to a Direct Unsubsidized Loan with interest.
For Relay students: Relay participates in the TEACH Grant program. Check with your Relay location’s financial aid contact to confirm your specific program’s eligibility. This is one of the few federal grants available at the graduate level—take advantage of it.

Federal Direct Unsubsidized Loans

Up to $20,500/yr Loan ▼
AmountUp to $20,500 per year for graduate students (unsubsidized only).
EligibilityEnrolled at least half-time in an eligible program. Complete the FAFSA.
Key DetailsInterest accrues during enrollment. Fixed interest rate set annually by Congress. Eligible for PSLF and income-driven repayment after graduation.
For Relay students: As an accredited nonprofit institution, Relay’s programs are eligible for federal financial aid. File your FAFSA as early as possible each year.

Graduate PLUS Loans

Up to cost of attendance Loan ▼
AmountUp to the full cost of attendance minus other financial aid received.
EligibilityEnrolled at least half-time; no adverse credit history; complete FAFSA.
Key DetailsHigher interest rate than Direct Unsubsidized Loans. Requires a credit check. Eligible for PSLF and IDR after consolidation.
For Relay students: Consider this only after exhausting Direct Unsubsidized Loans and the TEACH Grant. The higher interest rate means this should be a last resort for federal borrowing.

Segal AmeriCorps Education Award

~$7,395/yr • ~$14,790 for 2 yrs Award ▼
AmountOne year: ~$7,395 per full-time AmeriCorps term (2024–25 value, tied to max Pell Grant). Two years: ~$14,790 total for students who participate in AmeriCorps during each year of their Relay enrollment.
EligibilityMust complete an approved AmeriCorps term of service and enroll in the National Service Trust. Must use each award within 7 years of the service term’s completion.
Key DetailsCan be applied directly to tuition at Relay or used to repay qualified student loans. Taxable in the year used. AmeriCorps members may also be eligible to defer existing student loan payments during their service period.
For Relay students: Relay GSE operates its own AmeriCorps program. All Relay students enrolled for two years are eligible to participate in AmeriCorps for each of those years, earning two Segal Education Awards—up to ~$14,790 total—that can be applied directly to your Relay tuition. This is one of the most significant funding sources available to you; ask your Relay location about enrollment. If you also previously served with Teach For America, City Year, or another AmeriCorps program, you may have additional unused awards (up to the lifetime cap). Relay is a Title IV institution and accepts Segal Awards. Check your My AmeriCorps portal for your award status.

Federal Programs: After Graduation

These programs help reduce your loan burden once you are teaching. Plan for them now so you can take full advantage after completing your Relay program.

Public Service Loan Forgiveness (PSLF)

Full remaining balance Forgiveness ▼
AmountRemaining Direct Loan balance forgiven after 120 qualifying monthly payments (minimum 10 years). Tax-free.
EligibilityFull-time employment at a qualifying public school, government agency, or 501(c)(3) nonprofit. Direct Loans only. Must be on a qualifying repayment plan (income-driven repayment recommended).
Key DetailsNew PSLF rules effective July 1, 2026 narrow some employer eligibility, but public school teachers remain fully eligible. Cannot use the same teaching years for both PSLF and Teacher Loan Forgiveness, but you can use different years.
For Relay students: If you plan to teach in a public school or nonprofit school after graduation, PSLF is likely your most valuable forgiveness program. Start submitting your Employment Certification Form (now called the PSLF Form) as soon as you begin teaching. Enroll in an income-driven repayment plan to maximize the benefit.

Teacher Loan Forgiveness (TLF)

Up to $17,500 Forgiveness ▼
AmountUp to $17,500 for highly qualified math, science, or special education teachers; up to $5,000 for other qualifying teachers.
Eligibility5 consecutive years of full-time teaching at a school listed in the Teacher Cancellation Low Income (TCLI) Directory. Direct Subsidized/Unsubsidized or Stafford Loans. Must be “highly qualified.”
Key DetailsVerify your school appears in the TCLI Directory for all 5 years. Cannot use the same service years for both TLF and PSLF. Many Relay graduates qualify for the higher $17,500 amount due to teaching in special education or STEM fields.
ForbearanceWhile working toward TLF eligibility, you can apply for TLF loan forbearance, which allows you to pause your federal student loan payments during the qualifying teaching period. This can significantly reduce your out-of-pocket costs in the years immediately following program completion.
For Relay students: With Relay’s focus on high-need fields and partner schools in historically underserved communities, many Relay graduates are well-positioned for the maximum $17,500. Between TLF forbearance (pausing payments while you teach) and eventual forgiveness, your loans may cost you very little out of pocket after graduation. Consider completing TLF first (5 years), then pursuing PSLF with your remaining balance (using subsequent teaching years).

Federal Perkins Loan Cancellation

Up to 100% over 5 years Cancellation ▼
AmountUp to 100% cancellation over 5 years (15%, 15%, 20%, 20%, 30%). Includes interest that accrued during each year.
EligibilityMust hold Federal Perkins Loans (no new loans issued after Sept. 30, 2017, but existing loans remain eligible). Full-time teaching at a low-income school, or teaching special education, math, science, foreign languages, bilingual education, or a state-designated shortage area.
For Relay students: If you have Perkins Loans from a prior degree, do NOT consolidate them into a Direct Consolidation Loan if you plan to pursue Perkins cancellation—consolidation forfeits this benefit. Contact the school that issued your Perkins Loan to apply for cancellation.

Income-Driven Repayment (IDR) Forgiveness

Remaining balance after 20–25 yrs Forgiveness ▼
AmountRemaining balance forgiven after 20–25 years of qualifying payments, depending on the plan.
Key DetailsPayments based on income and family size. The new Repayment Assistance Plan (RAP) launches July 1, 2026. SAVE, PAYE, and ICR plans are being phased out by July 2028. Forgiveness may be taxable after 2025.
For Relay students: If you qualify for PSLF (10-year forgiveness), that is almost always the better path than waiting 20–25 years for IDR forgiveness. IDR is most useful as a safety net and for keeping payments low while you build your career.

Stacking Programs: A Smart Approach

The most effective way to reduce costs is to combine multiple programs. Here is an example pathway for a Relay student:

1
During enrollment: TEACH Grant (~$1,800/yr for part-time students) + Segal AmeriCorps Education Awards via Relay’s program (~$14,790 over 2 years) + applicable state grants or scholarships
↓
2
Years 1–5 teaching: Apply for TLF forbearance to pause loan payments while you teach. After 5 consecutive years at a TCLI school, apply for Teacher Loan Forgiveness ($5,000–$17,500)
↓
3
Years 6–10 teaching: Continue IDR payments at a qualifying employer. After 120 total payments, apply for PSLF (remaining balance forgiven, tax-free)
↓
4
Throughout: Apply for any applicable state programs (loan repayment, tax credits) to further reduce your balance while you work toward PSLF
Key stacking rules: Total aid cannot exceed your cost of attendance. You cannot use the same teaching service years for both TLF and PSLF (use years 1–5 for TLF, then years 6–10+ for PSLF). Always verify that specific programs allow concurrent receipt—some state programs prohibit overlap with certain federal programs.

The bottom line: students who plan ahead rarely pay full price.

Between federal grants, AmeriCorps awards through Relay’s own program, state-specific aid, employer tuition assistance, and post-graduation loan forgiveness, the actual amount a Relay student pays out of their own bank account is often a fraction of the listed tuition—and in many cases, approaches zero. Your Relay admissions and student services team can help you map out your personal funding plan before you enroll.

Other Potential Benefits

Two additional options may further offset costs depending on your situation, though they are not included in the core funding calculations above because they vary significantly by circumstance:

Employer tuition assistance: Some school districts offer tuition reimbursement or educational assistance under IRS Section 127 (up to $5,250/year tax-free). Availability varies widely by district. Ask your employing district’s HR department whether they offer this benefit for Relay coursework.

Lifetime Learning Credit: A non-refundable federal tax credit of up to $2,000/year for qualified education expenses. Because it reduces your tax liability rather than your tuition bill directly, it is not counted as a tuition offset—but it can lower your overall costs at tax time. Income limits apply. Consult a tax professional.

National Scholarships for Aspiring Teachers

Several national organizations offer scholarships for aspiring or current teachers: the NEA Foundation, Phi Delta Kappa, the James Madison Memorial Fellowship (secondary social studies), the Knowles Science Teaching Foundation (STEM teachers—up to $10,000/year for 2 years plus stipend), and professional associations in your subject area (NCTM, NCTE, NSTA, etc.).
